Underground Stations, Overground Stations, and Bus Services in London

Explore London's famous stations and easily find your way around the town with this handy guide to London stations.
Browse the list of London stations below to find out more about famous or lesser-known London stops.
There are lifts down to the platforms in all of the underground stations, or stairs for the fitness fanatics.

Covent Garden Station

This is a Central London tube station in the heart of Covent Garden area, close to Seven Dials and the Royal Opera House.

Farringdon Station

This station connects the main National Rail line station in Clerkenwell, central London. Just outside the City of London.

Stratford Station

It is a railway and underground station in East London. Queen Elizabeth Park is near the Station, as well as lots of hotels.

Barking Riverside Station

This new Overground station now connects passengers to Central London in a wonderfully-fast manner: as little as 22 minutes, and to Barking in 7 minutes.

Watford Junction to Euston

This Overground station takes an average of 33 minutes journey to the famous London Euston station. Tickets for the journey start from £11.30.

Romford to Upminster

This overground station is a railway branch line in London that connects Eastern Main Line to Upminster, Tilbury and Southend line, and London Underground.

Aldgate Bus Station

This station serves the Aldgate area of the City of London. The station is located directly in the south of Aldgate tube station.

Beckton Bus Station

This station is near Beckton, not too far from the London City Airport. Based in a U-shaped roadway, having on each arm a bus stop with shelter.

Edgware Bus Station

The bus station is off Station Road, situated a short distance away from Edgware Station and the Broadwalk Shopping Centre.
